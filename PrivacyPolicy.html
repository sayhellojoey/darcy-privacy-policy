Darcy Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 23, 2026

Darcy is designed as a local-first app for tracking personal health, appointments, and journey information.

By default, your records stay on your device. If you choose to turn on iCloud sync, eligible Darcy records can also sync through Apple's CloudKit service in your private iCloud account. Darcy does not run its own health-data backend, does not use advertising SDKs, and does not use third-party analytics SDKs to profile your activity.

What Darcy Stores

Darcy may store the following on your device:

  • Profile fields you choose to enter, such as name, birthday, and pronouns
  • Medication schedules, reminder settings, and intake history
  • Appointments
  • Journey notes, drawings, photos, and videos
  • Lab results and chart points
  • Song history and favorites used by Song Journey
  • Certain app settings and feature preferences, such as app lock and reminder options
  • Local Darcy Pro entitlement state so the app can remember unlocked features

If you export data, import data, create a journey media export, or create a backup, those files are created at your direction and stored wherever you choose to save them.

If you enable iCloud sync, eligible Darcy records can sync through Darcy's CloudKit container in your private iCloud account. Eligible sync data currently includes medications, intake history, journey entries, journey media, journey drawings, lab results, appointments, and song history.

Network and External Services

Darcy does not send your health records to our own servers.

Darcy may use network connections for the following:

  • Optional iCloud sync through Apple's CloudKit service in your private iCloud account
  • Darcy Pro purchase, restore, and entitlement verification through Apple's App Store and StoreKit services
  • Fetching curated song and discovery content from developer-managed GitHub-hosted files
  • Fetching a small remote pricing/config file for the early-adopter offer window

Those network requests do not intentionally include your health records. However, standard network metadata such as IP address and basic request information may be processed by Apple, GitHub, your ISP, or other infrastructure involved in delivering those requests.

On-Device and Apple-Provided Processing

Darcy performs most core tracking, reminder, and record-management work on device.

Darcy also uses Apple frameworks and services for certain features:

  • Face detection and alignment used by visual media features such as Evolution and face-centered presentation are processed on device with Apple's Vision framework
  • App lock uses your device's local authentication system, such as Face ID, Touch ID, or passcode
  • Speech dictation uses Apple's speech recognition stack; depending on your device, OS version, language, and Apple settings, dictation may be processed on device or by Apple
  • Calendar import reads events from Apple's EventKit framework when you choose to grant access

Darcy does not create or store its own biometric template for Face ID or Touch ID.

Permissions Darcy May Request

Darcy may ask for these permissions to enable specific features:

  • Biometrics: to unlock the app with Face ID or Touch ID
  • Photos: to pick existing images or videos for journey entries or profile images
  • Camera: to capture new journey or profile photos and videos
  • Photo Library Add Only: to save exported or selected media back to your photo library when you choose to do so
  • Microphone: for voice dictation and video capture audio
  • Speech Recognition: for note dictation and transcription
  • Calendar: to import events into Darcy appointments
  • Notifications: for medication and appointment reminders

You can change or revoke permissions at any time in iOS Settings.

Data Sharing

Darcy does not sell your personal data.

  • Darcy does not provide us with remote access to your private records.
  • Darcy does not require a Darcy account sign-in.
  • Darcy does not store your health records on developer-operated servers.

If you choose to use Apple services such as iCloud sync, App Store purchases, calendar import, or speech recognition, those features are governed in part by Apple's systems and policies.

If you choose to export, share, save, or back up your data, that content may contain sensitive information, and its later handling depends on where you save it, who you share it with, and what protections you use outside Darcy.

Data Security

Darcy relies on a combination of iOS protections and Darcy features to help protect your data, including local storage protections, optional app lock, encrypted backups, and user-controlled reminder privacy settings.

Encrypted backups use the password you choose. Darcy does not keep a copy of that password and cannot recover it for you.

Because Darcy data may be stored locally on your device and, if enabled, in your private iCloud account, your overall protection also depends on your device security, Apple account security, and operating system settings.

Your Controls

You can:

  • Export your data from Settings
  • Import a backup or data file from Settings
  • Turn iCloud sync on or off from Settings
  • Delete local Darcy data from this device from Settings
  • Manage permissions in iOS Settings

Important: turning off iCloud sync, switching a device to local-only mode, or deleting Darcy data on one device does not automatically delete copies that may already exist in your private iCloud account or on other synced devices.

Deleting the app removes locally stored Darcy data from that device, but it does not by itself guarantee removal of copies that may still exist in your private iCloud account.

Medical and HIPAA Disclaimer

Darcy is an organizational and tracking tool, not a medical device. Darcy does not provide diagnosis, treatment, or medical advice.

Darcy is not presented as a Covered Entity under HIPAA because Darcy does not receive, maintain, or transmit your protected health information to developer-operated backend systems. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional for medical decisions.

This policy may be updated as Darcy changes. The current version will be identified by the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy.
